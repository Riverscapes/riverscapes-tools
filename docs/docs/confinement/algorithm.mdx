The Confinement tool calculates the confinement and constriction ratios, as well as the confinement type (confined by the left or right valley margin or both) for a riverscape. The confinement of a riverscape is produced at several temporal scales. The confinement ratio is given at the individual stream level, at the stream reach level (100s of meters) and within the DGOs at the riverscapes reach scale. These metrics are calculated using an active channel area polygon and valley bottom extent polygon that are produced in the [Channel Area](https://tools.riverscapes.net/channelarea/) and Valley Bottom Extraction Tool [(VBET)](https://tools.riverscapes.net/vbet/). The confinement value for each stream or stream reach is calculated by finding where the active channel margins intersect or cross over the valley bottom polygon. This allows us to classify if the channel has a left confining margin, right confining margin, or is confined on both sides (constricted). The confinement ratio for the reach is calculated as
